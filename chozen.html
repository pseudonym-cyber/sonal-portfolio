chozen.
Product Case Study · 0→1 Concept

Choosing yourself shouldn't take courage.

chozen is a place to explore beauty & self-care rituals — and book them — without fear of being judged, and without fear of being harmed. Because the only thing standing between someone and the version of themselves they want should never be doubt.

RoleProduct — thesis, strategy, design, prototype
SurfaceMobile app (clickable prototype)
MarketIndia · UAE-validated category
StageConcept → high-fidelity prototype

A generation grew up watching beauty on Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat. They don't see microblading or lip blush as procedures. They see becoming — a fuller, more confident, more themselves version of who they already are.

And yet, when they go to actually do it, two things stop them: will I be judged for it, and is this even safe. chozen exists to remove both.

Nobody should have to defend wanting to feel like the best version of themselves — not before, not after. And nobody should have to gamble their face on whether the person, the tools, and the chemicals were safe.

— why I'm building chozen
The belief

It's not vanity. It's authorship.

Getting a ritual done isn't about being "fake," or fixing something that was wrong. It's a person deciding, on their own terms, to feel happier in their skin — and that choice deserves no apology. The taboo around it — "she got something done," "she didn't look good before" — is the real thing holding people back, more than money, more than time.

So chozen starts from a stance, not a feature list: your rituals are yours, chozen by you, no one else. We design every screen to make exploring feel private, normal, and celebrated — never something to be quietly ashamed of.

But you can't feel confident about a choice you don't feel safe making. Confidence requires trust. That single line is the whole product.

The problem, honestly

The category is booming. The trust isn't.

These services are everywhere on the feed and adored by the generation — but there is no place you can actually trust. Discovery is scattered across Instagram saves, WhatsApp referrals and Google reviews, and underneath the pretty grid sit two real fears no one is answering:

Fear one — being judged

"What will people think?"

The taboo is exhausting. People hide that they're even considering it, can't ask openly, and second-guess a choice that should feel joyful.

chozen reframes it as a ritual you choose for yourself — explored privately, spoken about warmly, never clinical or apologetic.

Fear two — being harmed

"Is this actually safe?"

Are the chemicals safe? Are the tools sterile? Does this person have real experience — and are they following the protocols these procedures legally demand? Today, you simply can't tell.

chozen verifies it for you: qualified professionals, hygiene & equipment standards, and protocol compliance — so the only thing you decide is that you want it.

The promise underneath both: you decide you want it. We take care of the rest — done right, safely, by the right hands.

Who I'm building for first

A 22-year-old, just starting out

The first person chozen is for is a woman in her early twenties who's just begun her career. She's fluent in aesthetics, comfortable online, and she wants these rituals — she sees them every day and they feel like part of becoming who she's growing into.

What stops her isn't desire. It's the fear of being judged, the fear of getting it wrong, and a price tag that feels like "luxury" on an early-career salary. chozen has to dissolve all three — and the last one matters more than people admit. Feeling like your best self shouldn't be gated by what you earn at 23.

How chozen delivers it

An experience built around removing fear

Every part of the flow maps to one of the three things in her way — judgment, safety, or cost. The journey is built around a single act, book the ritual, with each step quietly removing a reason to hesitate.

Splash→ Swipe to explore→ Your ritual / home→ Service→ Compare verified providers→ Provider profile→ Book in parts→ Confirmation

Explore without explaining yourself

A private, swipe-through deck of rituals replaces any quiz or interrogation. Curiosity costs nothing and asks nothing of you.

See it on your own face

A try-on lets you picture the outcome on yourself — privately — so the question "will it look right on me" gets answered before anyone else is involved.

Trust you can read at a glance

Every provider shows their qualification (PMU artist · dermatologist · cosmetologist), real attributed work, and that hygiene & protocol standards are met.

Access with dignity

Pay-in-parts sits on top of every ritual by default — so the right professional is reachable without it feeling like a luxury she has to be rich to deserve.

Product decisions

The choices, and why I made them

Each of these is a place where the obvious product move would have quietly reintroduced one of the three fears. So I chose differently.

Removing judgment

Swipe to explore — never a quiz

The obvious move

A 4-step onboarding quiz: budget, concern, goal, longevity.

What I did

A private swipe-through deck of rituals that quietly builds a personalised home.

Why: a quiz makes you declare and defend what you want before you're ready — the exact discomfort that keeps people away. Swiping lets curiosity stay private and pressure-free, and turns the same signal into delight instead of an interrogation.

Removing the cost barrier

Pay-in-parts as the default — not a price range

The obvious move

Show ₹15k–40k and let her filter by budget.

What I did

One confident price per ritual, with pay-in-parts (1 / 3 / 6) shown by default, 0% interest.

Why: a ₹30,000 number kills the feeling at the worst moment and whispers "this isn't for someone like you." "₹X a month" says the opposite — the right artist or dermatologist is within reach. It's a values choice about access, not a checkout feature. Ranges also signal uncertainty; one honest price signals trust.

Removing the fear of harm

Trust as the spine, not a badge

The obvious move

Marketplace-style: most listings, best prices, sort by rating.

What I did

Curate to verified providers and surface qualification, real work, hygiene & protocol compliance up front.

Why: this output is semi-permanent and lives on your face — safety isn't a filter, it's the entire promise. I'd rather show fewer providers I can vouch for than win on breadth. Trust is the moat and the hardest thing to fake, so it's designed in, not bolted on.

Matching how the decision is really made

Browse the outcome, then meet the person

The obvious move

Browse salons / providers first.

What I did

Ritual → see real results → compare who can deliver it → profile → book. The provider comes last.

Why: people commit to the outcome before they commit to a person. Leading with providers forces a trust decision too early. Leading with the result she wants — then showing who can safely deliver it — matches the actual order of the decision.

The category

Why these rituals, why now

I chose the services chozen launches with deliberately: the ones booming in the market and adored by this generation — microblading, lip blush, lash extensions, semi-permanent eyeliner, HydraFacials and the like — yet still homeless, with no trusted consumer brand sitting between the person and the provider.

  • ~40 real rituals across 6 categories — brows, lashes, lips, eyes, skin, PMU — each with real pricing, sessions and how long results last.
  • Outcome-first, not clinical. We lead with the feeling and the result, never the procedure name. Rituals, not treatments.
  • The UAE proves the appetite for a premium-but-approachable version of this category at scale; India is on the same path.
  • Repeat by nature — lash lifts, facials, touch-ups — which means trust, once earned, compounds.
What's next

If this were funded tomorrow

Phase 1 — Earn trust

One city, a small set of providers I can fully vouch for, the 6 core categories. Win on safety & confidence, not breadth.

Phase 2 — Real try-on

Ship the generative "see it on me." It's the most differentiated reason to open the app, and it removes doubt privately.

Phase 3 — Go deeper

Laser, Botox, fillers, PRP — higher-stakes rituals that only become possible once the trust is genuinely earned.

Why I built this

What it shows about how I work

I didn't start chozen from a feature or a market map. I started from something I believe — that wanting to feel like your best self is not something to be judged for, and that the only reason people hesitate is fear. Then I worked backwards: what would it take to remove the fear of judgment, the fear of harm, and the fear of cost — and built the product as the answer to those three.

That's how I think about product: find the real human reason someone stops, and design the specific thing that lets them keep going. The case for the category, the ICP, the service taxonomy from real pricing, the full flow, and a high-fidelity clickable prototype — I took all of it end to end myself, to make the belief real enough to feel, not just describe.
